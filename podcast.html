The Podcast

Still Here! Still Queer! Now What?

Conversations with queer people who have something to say and aren't done saying it. Candid, funny, occasionally filthy, always honest.

Start here

Not sure where to dive in? These five conversations are a good place to begin.

Charlie on the crush he didn't know was a crush

Michael J. Fox, the primary school jock, and what happens when you don't have the words yet. A conversation about early signals and late recognitions.

Duncan on the conversation he never had

What do you do when the person you most needed to tell is gone? Duncan told his dad he was gay when his dad was already in his coffin.

Gareth on being the generation nobody talks about

The queer people who survived Section 28 and AIDS — and then got quietly forgotten by the culture that moved on without them.

Peter on the shame that wasn't his

Conversion therapy at sixteen. Decades of carrying someone else's shame before realising it was never his to carry.

Grant on learning to like yourself

"Love yourself, bitch." At 55, Grant has opinions about bodies, pleasure, and what we owe ourselves.

Browse by theme

The conversations cross many themes, but these keep coming up.

Identity & selfhood

Who are you now? Who have you been? Conversations about intersectionality, neurodiversity, race, gender, and the parts of yourself you're still discovering.

Coming out — again and again

You don't come out once. You come out every time you start a new job, meet a new neighbour, or sit in a doctor's waiting room. These conversations are about the exhaustion and the courage of repetition.

Belonging & chosen family

Where do mid-life queer people fit? The scene has moved on, the apps aren't built for us, and the pubs are closing. But something else is emerging.

Ageing & bodies

Botox or not? Grey hair or not? Ageing is a privilege denied to many queer people before us — and it's also genuinely annoying. These conversations hold both truths.

From conversation to page

Some of the podcast conversations have been adapted as written pieces on the Journal. Start with The Crush You Didn't Know Was a Crush, drawn from my conversation with Charlie.
