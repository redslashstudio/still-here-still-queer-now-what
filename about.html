About the author

Dr Paul Taylor-Pitt

I've been a mushroom picker, shop assistant, prison psychologist, care assistant, head of HR and a coach. I have a doctorate I rarely mention in conversation and a husband called Dan who keeps me honest. We live in London with our plants, "the Sharons."

"I have no wish to grow old gracefully, but I hope I can do it gratefully."

For thirty years I worked in organisation development — helping people and institutions figure out how to change. I'm a Fellow of the Royal Society of Arts, a member of the Society of Authors, and I hold a doctorate in Organisation Development Professional Practice, a master's in HR Leadership, and an honours degree in psychology. I mention these not to impress but because I want you to know the thinking behind this book has rigour as well as heart.

How this book happened

Around my fiftieth birthday, something shifted. I'd spent years being what I call "hetero-normified" — the nice gay next door with the Mini Cooper and the Halloween decorations. Good job, good husband, good behaviour. And then I woke up.

I started asking myself what it actually means to be queer and middle-aged — not as a problem to solve, but as an identity to explore. I made a mid-life manifesto. I started a podcast. I spoke to over fifty queer people aged 40 to 60 and asked them everything: about shame, desire, ageing, coming out (again and again), chosen family, mortality, and what thriving might look like when the world still hasn't quite decided whether we're acceptable.

The book is what happened when I put all of that together.

The podcasts

I've created three podcasts: HOPECAST, Exploring The Heart Of Change, and Still Here! Still Queer! Now What? The Podcast! — which features conversations with queer people who have something to say and aren't done saying it.
